Tesla

Tesla Roadster

Tesla motors was founded in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Although Elon Musk is the current chairman of Tesla, his chairman journey began in 2008 when he became the first major investor of Tesla Motors. The first authentic vehicle lauch under the new leadership of Musk is the Tesla Roadster(as seen above)

Model S

The launch of the Tesla Roadster was a signficant leap for the market of EV vehicles but things didn't end there. Under Musk's leadership, the company grew signficantly and in the year 2012, Tesla launched the model S which was their entry into the mainstream car market

Model 3

In 2017 Tesla attempted to launch a more affordable eletric vehicle. They accomplished that with the launch of the model 3. With a starting price of 35k, the model 3 sought to compete with cars such as BMW's series 3 cars. The model 3 was a success and It quickly became a top selling eletric vehicle

Model X

Prior to the 2017 launch of the model 3, tesla attempted a quite bold endeavor into the suv market with their launch of the model X. The model X was designed to be a family car that had 7 seats available in 3 rows. One of the signature design choices of the model X came in Its falcon wing doors. The doors would open up in a vertical manner which adds a sort of uniqueness to the model X in comparison to other suv's

Model Y

With the continuous rise and popularity of crossover vehicles, Tesla sought to gain a piece of the pie with their model Y ev. Launched in 2020, Tesla's model Y was their take on a crossover which is basically a vehicle thats in between a sedan and a suv. Bigger than a sedan but smaller than an suv. With a range of 326 miles per charge and seats up to 7, the model Y Is a competitive vehicle in the crossover space
